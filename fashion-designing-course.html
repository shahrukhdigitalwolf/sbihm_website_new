Fashion Designing Course

Fashion Designing is one of the highly creative and in-demand careers in the world. It is especially for those who love to study fashion trends, sketch designs and develop something new and creative in the world of vogue. Wondering how to start a career in fashion designing? Well, you will need to pursue a basic design diploma or degree program to begin a career in this field which can help you learn the intricacies of designing clothes and apparel as well as studying the latest trends.

Fashion designing is the art of crafting customised apparel and lifestyle accessories and has turned into a career option now. It is a promising profession that is creative, and at the same time, serves a high package in the glamorous industry. However, it is a demanding sector, as one needs to be creative, and possess good managerial skills too. If you can create magic with shades, shapes, designs, cuts, and textiles, you have landed on the perfect article to stitch the decision that you have made a bit stronger.

Fashion Stylist

Fashion stylists plan and coordinate outfits to help clients look their best or showcase the clothing itself. They may select clothing for photo shoots, advertising campaigns, television and film productions or appearances made by public figures.

Fashion Designer

Fashion designers create original clothing and accessories, usually specializing in certain products. Depending on the designer, they may create items for mass production or more unique items for runways, boutiques and high-end stores.

Textile Designer

A textile designer designs 2D unique or repetitive patterns for printed fabrics, weave, and knit patterns. These fabrics may be used in the interiors to make clothes or as soft furnishing. Textile designers either work as a part of the team in industrial and non-industrial settings or even work independently. Most of the textile designers use CAD to design textures and patterns.

Public Relation Manager

Fashion public relations managers direct the public relations and communications departments for a company, ensuring to uphold its positive reputation.

Fashion Ecommerce Manager

Fashion e-commerce managers oversee a retail organization's online sales presence, with the ultimate goal of generating more purchases. Their regular responsibilities include reviewing sales data and inventory levels, creating pricing strategies and planning promotional campaigns.

Fashion brand Manager

Fashion brand managers oversee the maintenance and creation of a company's image with the goal of attracting and retaining customers. They collaborate with the creative, marketing and product development teams to ensure that all launches adhere to the brand's value and message.

Retail Buyer

Buyers watch the latest fashion trends and work with retail or department stores to select the products sold in their stores. The job may require buyers to attend trade shows and fashion shows or meet with designers to find products.

Art Director

In the fashion industry, art directors supervise the creation of visual materials for advertising campaigns, magazines and other media. They create the vision for the images and manage creative staff such as photographers, graphic designers and others to ensure they correctly execute the concept.

Fashion Marchandiser

Fashion merchandising managers make decisions regarding the stock, prices and presentation of products at retail stores. Day-to-day responsibilities include maintaining a store's visual appearance, coordinating sales promotions and checking inventory levels.

Sourcing manager

Sourcing managers in the fashion industry often work within the manufacturing sector and are responsible for keeping the process on schedule and cost-effective. They could be responsible for sourcing a variety of items for manufacturing clothing and accessories, including raw materials, fabrics and zippers.

Store Manager

A fashion product manager oversees the product development, design and sales teams through its manufacturing lifecycle from conception to release. Their role is also to ensure that the product aligns with the company's overall vision and goals.
